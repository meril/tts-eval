### 4Ô∏è‚É£ Naturalness (Human-ness)

**Listen for:**

- Natural breaths (not awkward gasps)
- Vocal texture consistency

**Good (4-5):** Sounds like a real person, warm voice, no weird artifacts
**Poor (1-2):** Robotic quality, tinny/buzzy, clicks, unnatural breaths

**Note:** This is HOLISTIC - it's the "gut feel" of human-ness

**Good (4-5):** Rock solid throughout
**Poor (1-2):** Quality drops, speed changes, voice breaks

## üéØ Prosody vs Naturalness - THE KEY DISTINCTION

These are the most confusing! Here's the difference:

| Aspect | Prosody | Naturalness |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **Focus** | Melody/rhythm of speech | Overall human-ness |
| **Scope** | Specific (pitch, timing, stress) | Holistic (voice quality, artifacts) |
| **Decomposable?** | Yes (can point to specific words) | No (overall perception) |

## üìù Evaluation Workflow

### Step 1: First Listen (Pronunciation)
Play both samples once, focus on:
- Are words pronounced correctly?
- Any obvious mispronunciations?

### Step 2: Second Listen (Prosody & Emotion)
Play again, focus on:
- Does the rhythm feel natural?
- Is emphasis on the right words?
- Does tone match the content?

### Step 3: Third Listen (Naturalness & Consistency)
Play one more time:
- Does it sound human overall?
- Any artifacts, clicks, weird breaths?
- Quality stable throughout?

### Step 4: Compare Directly
Play both back-to-back to decide:
- Which sounds better overall?
- What specific differences do you hear?

### Step 5: Take Notes!
Record specific observations:
- "0:03 - weird pause after 'lead'"
- "breath at 0:15 sounds unnatural"
- "emphasis wrong on 'guitarist'"

## üéì Common Failure Modes

### Heteronyms (E1 tests)
**What to check:** Is context-dependent pronunciation correct?
- "lead" singer (leed) vs "lead" the band (led)
- "read" past (red) vs "read" future (reed)

### Numbers (E2 tests)
**What to check:** How are numbers/dates spoken?
- "4/5/67" ‚Üí Should it be a date or a fraction?
- Phone numbers ‚Üí Natural grouping?
- Percentages ‚Üí Clear and correct?

### Medical (E4 tests)
**What to check:** Complex terminology
- Drug names: adalimumab, ipratropium
- Diseases: cytomegalovirus pneumonitis
- Empathy in diagnosis delivery

### Prosody (E6 tests)
**What to check:** Long-form naturalness
- Does quality hold for 30+ seconds?
- Emotional shifts handled well?
- Narrative flow feels natural?

## üí° Pro Tips

1. **Listen with headphones** - You'll catch more artifacts
2. **Don't overthink it** - Trust your gut reaction
3. **Compare is key** - Play both samples back-to-back
4. **Take breaks** - Listening fatigue is real (every 10 tests)
5. **Note timestamps** - "0:15 - weird breath" is more useful than "sounds off"
6. **Default to 3** - Use full scale: 1=terrible, 3=okay, 5=perfect
7. **Notes are gold** - Future-you will appreciate specifics
